particle moving on the astroid at constant frequency


In parametric Cartesian equations, the astroid can be represented by

x=a⁢cos3⁡ω⁢t,y=a⁢sin3⁡ω⁢t,

where a>0 is a known constant, ω>0 is the constant angular frequency, and t∈[0,∞) is the time parameter. Thus the position vector of a particle, moving over the astroid, is

𝐫=a⁢cos3⁡ω⁢t⁢𝐢+a⁢sin3⁡ω⁢t⁢𝐣,

and its velocity

𝐯=-3⁢a⁢ω⁢sin⁡ω⁢t⁢cos2⁡ω⁢t⁢𝐢+3⁢a⁢ω⁢sin2⁡ω⁢t⁢cos⁡ω⁢t⁢𝐣,

where {𝐢,𝐣} is a reference basis. Hence for the particle speed we have

v=3⁢a⁢ω⁢sin⁡ω⁢t⁢cos⁡ω⁢t.

From the last two equations we get the tangent vector

𝐓=-sin⁡ω⁢t⁢𝐢+cos⁡ω⁢t⁢𝐣,

and by using the well known formula 11By applying the chain rule, ∥d⁢𝐓d⁢t∥=∥d⁢𝐓d⁢s∥⁢|d⁢sd⁢t|=∥𝐍ρ∥⁢v=vρ, by Frenet-Serret. 𝐍 is the normal vector.

∥d⁢𝐓d⁢t∥=vρ,

ρ>0 being the radius of curvature at any instant t, we arrive to the useful equation

v=ω⁢ρ.
